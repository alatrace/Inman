Building the team for what comes next · 2025–2026

July 2025

Data + intelligence

Mike Simonsen

Chief Economist — first in company history

Founded Altos Research in 2006, building real-time MLS tools tracking days on market and price reductions. Now runs market intelligence for Compass — the brokerage that argues that same data hurts sellers.

His weekly market update series, once under HousingWire, now runs under the Compass brand.

August 2025

Finance + operations

Scott Wahlers

Chief Financial Officer

Joined Compass in 2018 as Chief Accounting Officer. Helped orchestrate the 2022 restructuring that cut over $600M in operating expenses. Elevated to CFO when Reelitz departed — just as the Anywhere deal was taking shape.

A seven-year Compass insider promoted at a pivotal moment, fitting the pattern of Reffkin elevating loyalists as the company scaled.

September 2025

Legal + antitrust

Ethan Glass

Chief Legal Officer

Led DOJ teams that investigated MLS organizations for anticompetitive conduct. Then became NAR's lead antitrust attorney in Sitzer/Burnett. Now brings that knowledge — from both sides — to Compass's fights against MLSs and Zillow.

Hired the same month Anywhere was announced. Had already been working with Compass on the NWMLS lawsuit before joining full time.

February 2026

Culture + integration

Neda Navab

President, Compass

Started as Reffkin's chief of staff in 2018, rose to eastern region president, now leads the brokerage. Her mandate: make the world's largest brokerage feel like a boutique across all 340,000 agents and every brand.

"Big is our engine, but boutique is our edge." — to Inman, March 2026

March 2026

Operations + brands

Sue Yannaccone

Chief Operating Officer, Compass International Holdings

Spent 11 years at Anywhere, where she publicly criticized the private listings push as an effort to "concentrate listings" at consumers' expense. Now COO of the company that acquired Anywhere and runs the industry's largest private listings program.

"My sole focus is ensuring that every one of our 340,000 real estate professionals is benefiting directly from the strength of our combined organization." — to Inman, March 2026

Rory Golod
