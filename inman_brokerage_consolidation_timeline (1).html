March 15, 2024Regulatory backdrop
NAR — Proposed $418M settlement announced
Sets up industrywide rule changes and new liability exposure for brokerages and agents. The starting gun for the consolidation era. Rule changes would take effect Aug. 17, 2024.

Rocket / Redfin — merger agreement signed
Agreement and Plan of Merger executed March 9 and filed with the SEC. Publicly announced via joint press release the following morning, March 10. The one-day gap between signing and announcement is confirmed by SEC filings from both companies.

March 10, 2025Announced
Rocket Companies to acquire Redfin
All-stock deal, $1.75B equity value. Combines Rocket Mortgage — America's largest mortgage lender — with Redfin's search platform and ~50M monthly visitors. Rocket frames it as connecting search, agents and mortgage in a single consumer funnel. Closed July 1, 2025.

March 13, 2025Reported; denied
Compass / HomeServices of America — talks reported, then denied
WSJ reports Compass in "advanced talks" to acquire HomeServices of America, including Berkshire Hathaway HomeServices. Hours later, HomeServices CEO Gino Blefari categorically denies it: "No discussions, negotiations or agreements." Deal never materializes. The report came the same week as the Rocket/Redfin announcement — illustrating how fast the consolidation rumor mill was moving in March 2025.

2025 — Q2
May 23, 2025Approach reported; no deal
Anywhere makes reported unsolicited bid for Douglas Elliman
Bloomberg reports Anywhere offered $4+/share — roughly double Elliman's stock price at the time. Sources say Elliman was unlikely to accept. Neither company confirmed or denied the report. The approach came shortly after Anywhere named a new M&A lead and signaled acquisition ambitions. Four months later, Anywhere itself was acquired by Compass.

Sept. 22, 2025Announced
Compass to acquire Anywhere Real Estate
All-stock transaction, ~$10B enterprise value including debt. Compass shareholders to own ~78%, Anywhere ~22%. Brands to remain separate: Coldwell Banker, Century 21, Sotheby's International Realty, Corcoran, ERA and Better Homes and Gardens Real Estate. Originally projected to close H2 2026; closed Jan. 9, 2026 — months ahead of schedule.

2025 — Q4
Oct. 16, 2025Closed
Howard Hanna acquires Elegran Real Estate
Enters Manhattan. Howard Hanna becomes the largest brokerage in New York State by agent count and homes sold. Elegran CEO Michael Rossi stays on. Howard Hanna now connects NYC through Long Island, Westchester, Connecticut, Hudson Valley and Northern New Jersey. The company describes itself as the nation's largest privately held brokerage and fifth-largest overall.

2026 — Q1
Jan. 9, 2026Closed
Compass — Anywhere acquisition closes
Closes months ahead of projected H2 2026 timeline. ~99% of Compass votes in favor; ~72% of Anywhere votes in favor. Anywhere survives as a wholly owned Compass subsidiary; HOUS delisted from NYSE. Combined company: ~340,000 agents across ~120 countries. Compass issues $1B in convertible notes to retire Anywhere debt. Confirmed by SEC 8-K.

April 26, 2026Agreement signed
Real / REMAX — definitive agreement signed
Arrangement Agreement and Plan of Merger executed April 26 and filed with the SEC. Publicly announced the following morning, April 27. The one-day gap between signing and announcement mirrors the Rocket/Redfin pattern and is confirmed by SEC filings from both companies.

April 27, 2026Announced; pending
Real Brokerage to acquire REMAX Holdings
$880M enterprise value. Creates "Real REMAX Group." Real shareholders to own ~59%, REMAX shareholders ~41%. Combines Real's AI/cloud platform with REMAX's ~8,500 franchises, ~145,000 agents and Motto Mortgage. Real CEO Tamir Poleg to lead combined company, headquartered in Miami. $550M financing commitment from Morgan Stanley and Apollo. REMAX carried $436.8M in outstanding debt as of Dec. 31, 2025. Close expected H2 2026, pending regulatory and shareholder approval.

May 7, 2026Closed
eXp World Holdings acquires NextHome
Announced and closed simultaneously. Financial terms undisclosed; eXp used cash on hand. Adds 500+ NextHome franchisees. eXp transitions to a "multi-model platform" supporting both cloud brokerage and franchise models. Nasdaq ticker changes from EXPI to AGNT effective May 8. NextHome co-CEOs James Dwiggins and Keith Robinson remain as President and President of Strategy. Confirmed by SEC 8-K.
